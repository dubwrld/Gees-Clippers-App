Blood pressure refers to the force of blood against the walls of arteries as the heart pumps it around the body. It is a crucial physiological measurement that helps assess the health of the cardiovascular system. Blood pressure is typically expressed as two numbers, like a fraction: systolic pressure over diastolic pressure.

    Blood sugar, also known as blood glucose, refers to the amount of sugar (glucose) present in your bloodstream. Glucose is a type of sugar that serves as the primary source of energy for cells throughout your body. It is derived from the carbohydrates you consume in your diet.



    Maintaining balanced blood sugar levels is essential for overall health and proper bodily functioning. The body tightly regulates blood sugar levels to ensure a steady supply of energy to cells while preventing excessively high or low levels, which can lead to health complications.
